Plot Summary

Lucky Bhaskar tells the story of Bhaskar Kumar, a lower-middle-class bank cashier in 1980s India who, frustrated by inequality and systemic corruption, risks everything by entering the illegal world of stock market trading. His rise to wealth brings fame and fear as the CBI chases him and his marriage begins to fall apart. The film explores the cost of ambition, moral compromise, and redemption.

  • Dulquer Salmaan as Bhaskar Kumar, the pivotal bank cashier who transforms into a con‑artist driven by desperation and ambition.
  • Meenakshi Chaudharyas Sumathi Kumar, Bhaskar’s loyal but conflicted wife, whose relationship with him is tested by his new double life.
  • Supporting cast includes Tinnu Anand, P. Sai Kumar (as the CBI officer), Sachin Khedekar, Ramki, Raghu Babu, and Y. Kasi Viswanath, all adding depth to the conspiracy, family, and legal drama.

“When life doesn’t give you a break, sometimes you have to take it.” This line, delivered by Dulquer’s Bhaskar at a critical turning point, encapsulates his desperation—and the moral ambiguity at the heart of his choices.
