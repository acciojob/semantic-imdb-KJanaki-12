Lucky Baskhar

Summary:Set in late‑1980s Mumbai, the story follows Bhaskar (played by Dulquer Salmaan), a lower‑middle‑class bank cashier who struggles with mounting debts and humiliation at work. After being repeatedly denied a promotion despite his hard work, he takes a drastic risk—embezzling funds from his bank. He dives into the shady world of illegal stock investments and money laundering, teaming up with a powerful stock broker inspired by figures like Harshad Mehta. As wealth pours in, Bhaskar’s life spirals: his marriage with Sumathi (played by Meenakshi Chaudhary) strains, and eventually, the CBI zeroes in on his scams. He must navigate betrayal, guilt, and legal peril to attempt redemption and accountability by the film’s climax.

  • Dulquer Salmaan as Bhaskar Kumar, the pivotal bank cashier who transforms into a con‑artist driven by desperation and ambition.
  • Meenakshi Chaudharyas Sumathi Kumar, Bhaskar’s loyal but conflicted wife, whose relationship with him is tested by his new double life.
  • Supporting cast includes Tinnu Anand, P. Sai Kumar (as the CBI officer), Sachin Khedekar, Ramki, Raghu Babu, and Y. Kasi Viswanath, all adding depth to the conspiracy, family, and legal drama.

“When life doesn’t give you a break, sometimes you have to take it.” This line, delivered by Dulquer’s Bhaskar at a critical turning point, encapsulates his desperation—and the moral ambiguity at the heart of his choices.
